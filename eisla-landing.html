Why "Eisla"?

Named after the inventor of the PCB.

Where Eisler believed electronics should be printed, not wired — we believe they should be described, not designed.
In 1936, an Austrian refugee named Paul Eisler sat in a London boarding house with a radical idea: what if electronics could be printed rather than hand-wired?

He built the world's first printed circuit board — the invention that makes every modern electronic device possible. He sold the rights for £1 and wasn't recognised as the inventor for 35 years.

Eisla carries his name forward. Tell us what you need in plain English. Our AI handles the engineering. A qualified engineer reviews the design. Your board arrives at your door.

The copper in our brand is the copper on every PCB. The name is a promise that Eisler's vision — electronics accessible to everyone — is finally being fulfilled.
Common questions

Everything you need to know.

Do I need any engineering knowledge?

None at all. You describe what you need in plain English — “a motion sensor with BLE that runs on a coin cell” — and we handle the entire design process. The review step shows you a 3D render and a plain-English summary, not schematics.

What if I need changes to my design?

During the review step you can request changes by simply describing what you want different. Need an extra sensor? A different connector? Just say so. We iterate until you're happy before manufacturing.

Who owns the design files?

You do, outright. You receive all Gerber and KiCad files with every order. Take them to any manufacturer, modify them, or use them however you like. No lock-in, no licence fees.

How long does the whole process take?

Design typically takes 1–3 days depending on complexity. Manufacturing and delivery adds 5–10 business days depending on which fab you choose. End to end, most customers have boards in hand within 1–2 weeks.
